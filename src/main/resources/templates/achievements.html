ISRO

Chandrayaan-1 and Chandrayaan-2 represent significant achievements for ISRO in lunar exploration. Chandrayaan-1, launched in 2008, discovered water molecules on the Moon's surface, transforming our lunar knowledge. It also confirmed the existence of water ice in lunar polar regions. Chandrayaan-2, launched in 2019, aimed to further our understanding by including an orbiter, a lander (Vikram), and a rover (Pragyan). While Vikram's attempted landing didn't go as planned, the orbiter continues to study the Moon and has made valuable discoveries. Both missions showcased India's space capabilities, contributing to global lunar research and highlighting ISRO's commitment to space exploration.

"Aryabhata," an ancient Indian mathematician and astronomer who made significant contributions to the fields of mathematics and astronomy. Aryabhata used a decimal number system, which included place value notation and the concept of zero, long before they were introduced in Europe. This was a groundbreaking contribution to mathematics. Vedas

The Vedas are a collection of ancient sacred texts that are among the most revered scriptures in India. There are four main Vedas in India, each with its own set of texts and hymns: Rigveda: The Rigveda is the oldest and most important of the Vedas. Yajurveda: The Yajurveda contains rituals and guidelines for performing various sacrificial ceremonies (yajnas) and rituals. Samaveda: The Samaveda is primarily a collection of melodies and chants used in rituals and ceremonies. Atharvaveda: The Atharvaveda contains hymns and mantras that are more diverse in nature compared to the other Vedas.

The Human Computer

Shakuntala Devi, often referred to as the "Human Computer," was an Indian mathematician, writer, and mental calculator known for her extraordinary abilities in mental mathematics. Shakuntala Devi authored several books, including "Figuring: The Joy of Numbers," in which she shared her insights into mathematics and mental calculation techniques. he set numerous world records for her mental calculation abilities.Shakuntala Devi's talents took her around the world, where she performed on television shows, in universities, and at public events. Sushruta is the father of surgery

Sushruta is the father of surgery. If the history of science is traced back to its origin, it probably starts from an unmarked era of ancient time. Although the science of medicine and surgery has advanced by leaps and bounds today, many techniques practiced today have still been derived from the practices of the ancient Indian scholars. ushruta has described surgery under eight heads: Chedya (excision), Lekhya (scarification), Vedhya (puncturing), Esya (exploration), Ahrya (extraction), Vsraya (evacuation), and Sivya (suturing). All the four Vedas are in the form of Shlokas (hymns), verses, incantations, and rites in Sanskrit language. This treatise contains detailed descriptions of teachings and practice of the great ancient surgeon Sushruta and has considerable surgical knowledge of relevance even today.

Olympics

India's Olympic journey is a testament to the nation's dedication to sports and athletic excellence. Since its debut at the Paris Olympics in 1900, India has made significant strides in the international sporting arena. Notably, the country's prowess in field hockey during the mid-20th century resulted in an impressive haul of gold medals, with victories in London (1948), Helsinki (1952), Melbourne (1956), and Rome (1960). However, the modern era has seen India diversify its sporting pursuits. The emergence of athletes like Abhinav Bindra, who secured India's first individual gold in shooting at the 2008 Beijing Olympics, and the success of weightlifter Mirabai Chanu in Tokyo 2020, have marked a shift towards a broader representation in various sports. Despite facing challenges such as limited infrastructure, funding issues, and stiff competition, Indian athletes continue to make their mark on the global stage. The Indian government and various sports bodies are increasingly investing in grassroots development, sports academies, and international exposure for athletes, paving the way for a promising future for India in the Olympics. As India's athletes shine on the world stage, they inspire the nation and demonstrate that with determination and support, India's Olympic legacy is set to grow even richer in the years to come.

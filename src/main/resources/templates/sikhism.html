Sikkism

Sikkism is a distinct religion that originated in the Punjab region of India in the late 15th century. It was founded by Guru Nanak Dev Ji and emphasizes the belief in one God and the importance of selfless service, equality, and devotion. Sikhs follow the teachings of ten successive Gurus, with Guru Granth Sahib being their central religious scripture.

Sikhs are recognized by their distinctive attire, including turbans and uncut hair. They practice regular prayer, meditation, and community service. Sikkism promotes a strong sense of community and equality, rejecting caste distinctions and advocating for social justice and humanitarian efforts.
